What is Recursion?
Recursion is a programming technique in which a method calls itself to solve a problem. Instead of solving the entire problem at once, the problem is divided into smaller parts, and the same method is used repeatedly until a stopping condition is reached.

A recursive solution has two parts:
Base Case: The condition where recursion stops.
Recursive Case: The method calls itself with a smaller input.
Example

Suppose the present amount is ₹10,000 and it grows by 10% every year.
Year 0 : ₹10,000
Year 1 : ₹11,000
Year 2 : ₹12,100
Year 3 : ₹13,310
Instead of calculating everything manually, recursion repeatedly calculates the value for the previous year and then adds the growth.
